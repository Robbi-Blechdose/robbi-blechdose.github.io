MX-5 KITT: Central locking

2020-12-08

As the MX-5 will be my daily driver, there are a few convenience mods I’m planning. Chief among these is a feature so standard on modern cars noone pays any attention to it: remote central locking.

Even though the MX-5 only has two doors, it’s still unnerving to have to use the key on both doors every time, and popping the trunk with the key gets old fast when you go shopping.

Luckily, I.L. Motorsport has a kit just for this purpose, and I got the boot release as well. These kits are essentially rebranded Chinesium, but the quality is good and most importantly, I.L. adds mounting brackets, which make up the actual value of the kit. With these, the install is relatively straightforward.

I started with the boot release mechanism: Simply take the carpet in front of the lock off, then put in the mounting bracket and motor, and add the rod which connects to the standard rod (which is used when turning the key in the lock).

Next up were the doors. These too were relatively easy: Take off the door cards, peel back the plastic sheet (also called vapor barrier) a bit (while trying not to get the black goo on your hands, which I of course failed to do). Then drill four holes, put the motor on the bracket and connect the rod, then finally put the bracket in.

The worst part of the install is threading the wires from the motor through the rubber grommet between the door and body.
Once that’s done, you just need to choose a spot for the control box which mounts with double-sided tape (provided in the box). Then all that’s left to do is to connect the box to permanent power and you’re done!
