MX-5 KITT: Center console

2021-02-24

In order to give the car a bit more of the sweet 80s retro-futurism vibes, I started work on a new center console piece.
Normally, there is an unused spot between the shifter and the power window switches (well, it contains and ashtray, but that is essentially unused space). I decided to design an inset which would replace the ashtray.
The idea was to add controls for a scanner and a few other useful bits and bobs. I settled on a QuickCharge port for a phone, a temperature display and a clock.

Next up were the electronics. The clock is easy with the help of a battery-backed RTC board (even though there’s quite a bit of drift).
The temperature display uses a BME280 (or BMP280, I never can remember).
Everything is controlled by an ESP32, which also gives me the option to press a button and make it connect to my phone’s WiFi hotspot to update the time.

Once the CNC’ed faceplate was done, I put the electronics in:

One of the most recognizable bits KITT had was the “scanner”, that very 80s red LED strip at the front.
The center console is in charge of that as well.

For that, there are 6 LEDs, a button to turn it on and a thumbwheel switch to select the mode:

  • KITT
  • Warning flash
  • Eco
  • American police strobe lights
  • Pink
  • RGB (that one animation which fades through all colors)

There was a handy unused grommet behind the glovebox which allowed me to thread the cables through to the front of the car where I installed the LED strip.
